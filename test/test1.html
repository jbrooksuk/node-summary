Meet the 23-Year-Old Innovating The Nail Industry With Static Nails
Vivienne Decker i

Alexis Irene is the 23-year-old founder of Static Nails, a manicure company she created out of her college dorm room. Static Nails is an at-home nail kit, featuring reusable nail appliqués which are applied using their non-damaging glue and can be worn up to 18 days, or reapplied up to 6 times. Launched in 2016, Static Nails earned $500,000 in sales in the first 6 months of business, partnering with retailers including Sephora and HSN.

“Five years ago, I had my first innovative idea that would fix everything women hated about manicures,” Alexis Irene said. “At that time, painting your ring finger a different color had become the trend and nail art was about to boom. I was flipping through Instagram when I came across these intricate 3D doughnut nails and that’s when my idea for Static Nails hit me.

Alexis Irene, founder of Static NailsClif Ellis

“I realized that if you wanted jaw-dropping nail art, it’s not easily accessible unless you live in Hollywood. Finding a really talented manicurist is hard to come by, and getting your nails done was time consuming, smelled bad, was damaging to your nails, and cost a ton. As a result of all this time, money, and effort your polish would smudge then chip two days later, or you’d be paying megabucks to damage your natural nails to maintain gels and acrylics.”

Irene felt that at that time, the only options for press-ons nails were drug store brands either came in a limited colors, or featured a French-tip style. 

“I took this opportunity to turn an overlooked category into a must-have fashion accessory,” Irene said. “To do so, I created the name, Pop-Ons, to disassociate ourselves with drugstore press-ons, which the majority of women disliked from a product quality standpoint.”

Static NailsStatic Nails

Irene launched Static Nails with money she had saved from modeling and working as a makeup artist. She was quickly able to secure a brand partnership with Swarovski, and began working on the first press-on type nail to have 3D objects, intricate prints, and special effects.

“Static’s Reusable Pop-On Nails give everyone all over the world access to luxury, on-trend nail art for a fraction of the money and time spent in salons," Irene said. "The best part is that these nails are non-damaging to natural nails, they can be customized without damaging the original design, they only take 5 minutes to apply, and can be worn up to 18 days, or reapplied up to 6 times.”


Static Nails Static Nails

Alexis Irene launched Static Nails on her 21st birthday, and after being in business for one week, she received a call from Kendo, the beauty brand incubator behind Ole Henriksen and Marc Jacobs Beauty.

“James Vincent, a celebrity makeup artist introduced our product to tons of designers, which resulted in us doing lots of New York Fashion Week shows,” Irene said. “Static was featured in almost every well-known magazine. Top beauty and fashion editors from Teen Vogue to Cosmopolitan even rocked Static Nails during the runway shows. I also received calls from major retailers, but had to sadly decline as I felt the product wasn’t quite ready for mass retail as I was also still in school.  It was such an amazing feeling to experience two years of hard work, figuring out how to start a company and develop a new product, to then seeing it take off. I had put my college funds on the line to get the company off the ground and risked not being able to complete my senior year if the company failed.”

Static NailsStatic Nails

On Irene’s 22nd birthday, she got a fateful call from a blocked number.

“It was one of Sephora’s PR agents, telling me how obsessed she was with the nails and that she had passed on a good word,” Irene said. “Best birthday present ever. We started chatting with Sephora a few months later for Static Nails to be picked-up in-store.”

Static NailsStatic Nails

Irene's background of working in the beauty industry prepared her to become a beauty entrepreneur.

"I started working in the beauty industry at age 14 as a model," Irene said. "At age 16, I was the youngest professionally signed makeup artist in the country at the time. By the time I turned 17, I was Macy’s Corporation’s youngest Cosmetic Manager ever in one of their top performing stores, the Bloomingdale's in Santa Monica."

Irene was also fortunate to have worked with several brands under the L'Oreal umbrella, where she learned about trade show coordination, buyer meetings, product launches and product formulations.

"Working with such established brands and the wonderful, creative and driven people behind them from the beauty counters to the corporate offices helped me figure out what women really wanted, what worked and what didn’t," Irene said."

Static NailsStatic Nails

Now that Irene is out of college, she's in the process of scaling up Static Nails.

"My goal is for Static Nails to be the number one consumer nail brand," Irene said. "So over the next year we will be launching new, innovative products that produce insane results. Some products have been in the works for over two years so I’m very excited to release them."
